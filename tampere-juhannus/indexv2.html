A Small Event With A Bigger Purpose

On 19 June 2026, while many people across Finland were preparing for Midsummer celebrations, a small group of volunteers gathered in Tampere with a different objective.

Rather than focusing solely on the holiday itself, they chose to dedicate part of their day to improving public spaces and giving something back to the community.

The Tampere Juhannus Cleanup was created around a simple belief: cleaner cities are built by people who care enough to take action.

Although only four volunteers participated, the event was never about the number of people involved.

Instead, it was about demonstrating that positive change often begins with ordinary people taking practical action.

Together, volunteers collected approximately six kilograms of litter while encouraging conversations about environmental responsibility, public spaces, and community engagement.

The event was supported by Tyozy, a Tampere-based cleaning and moving company that believes businesses can contribute positively to the communities they serve.

Supporting volunteer-led initiatives is one way of extending those values beyond commercial services.

While six kilograms may seem like a modest result, the true value of the event extends far beyond the weight of collected waste.

Every item removed represents a small improvement to the environment and a reminder that shared spaces require shared responsibility.

Pieni Tapahtuma, Suurempi Tarkoitus

Vaikka osallistujia oli vain muutama, tapahtuman tarkoitus oli osoittaa, että positiivinen muutos voi alkaa pienestä ryhmästä ihmisiä.

Vapaaehtoiset keräsivät noin kuusi kiloa roskaa ja samalla lisäsivät tietoisuutta ympäristövastuusta sekä yhteisten alueiden merkityksestä.

Tapahtumaa tuki Tyozy, joka haluaa edistää puhtaampia yhteisöjä ja vastuullista toimintaa.

Why We Organized The Tampere Juhannus Cleanup

Juhannus is one of Finland's most important and beloved celebrations.

Across the country, people gather with friends and family, travel to summer cottages, spend time by lakes, enjoy saunas, and celebrate the brightest days of the year.

Public spaces play an important role during these celebrations.

Parks, waterfronts, walking paths, beaches, and gathering places become locations where memories are created and communities come together.

Unfortunately, increased activity can sometimes lead to increased litter.

The purpose of this cleanup was never to criticize people enjoying the holiday.

Instead, the goal was to encourage a culture where everyone feels responsible for preserving the spaces they enjoy.

We wanted to focus on action rather than criticism.

The question was simple: What can we do today to make Tampere a little better?

The answer became this event.

Miksi Tapahtuma Järjestettiin

Juhannus on yksi Suomen tärkeimmistä juhlista.

Ihmiset viettävät aikaa puistoissa, rannoilla ja muilla yhteisillä alueilla.

Tapahtuman tavoitteena ei ollut arvostella juhlintaa vaan kannustaa huolehtimaan yhteisestä ympäristöstä.

Ajatus oli yksinkertainen: mitä voimme tehdä tänään tehdäksemme Tampereesta hieman paremman?

Why Tampere Is Worth Protecting

Tampere is one of Finland's most vibrant and livable cities.

Situated between two large lakes, the city combines natural beauty, industrial heritage, innovation, culture, and strong community spirit.

Residents and visitors enjoy parks, waterfront routes, beaches, cycling paths, forests, and public gathering spaces throughout the year.

During summer these places become even more important.

Keeping them clean helps preserve what makes Tampere such a welcoming and enjoyable city.

Community cleanups are therefore not only environmental projects.

They are investments in the future experience of everyone who lives in or visits the city.

Miksi Tampereesta Kannattaa Pitää Huolta

Tampere tunnetaan järvistään, puistoistaan ja aktiivisesta kaupunkikulttuuristaan.

Environmental Impact Report

One of the easiest mistakes when evaluating a cleanup event is focusing exclusively on the total weight of collected litter.

While approximately six kilograms of waste were removed during the Tampere Juhannus Cleanup, the true impact extends far beyond a single number.

Every item removed from public spaces represents a potential environmental problem prevented.

Plastic packaging left outdoors can eventually break down into microplastics. Food containers can attract animals and create sanitation issues. Discarded materials may enter drainage systems and eventually reach waterways.

By removing litter before it remains in the environment for extended periods, volunteers help reduce these risks while improving the appearance and usability of shared public spaces.

Environmental responsibility often begins with practical action. Community cleanups provide a direct and visible way for individuals to contribute to environmental protection at a local level.

Ympäristövaikutukset

Kuusi kiloa kerättyä roskaa on vain yksi tapa mitata tapahtuman vaikutuksia.

Jokainen kerätty roska vähentää ympäristökuormitusta ja parantaa yhteisten alueiden viihtyisyyttä.

Paikalliset ympäristöteot voivat olla pieniä, mutta niiden vaikutukset ovat todellisia ja näkyviä.

🌿 Cleaner Public Spaces

Removing litter immediately improves the appearance and usability of parks, walkways, and gathering areas.

Collected waste is prevented from reaching waterways and breaking down into harmful pollutants.

Media, Visibility & Awareness

One of the most valuable outcomes of community events is awareness.

Every person who notices volunteers collecting litter is exposed to a simple but powerful message: public spaces matter.

Community cleanups create visible examples of positive action.

Rather than focusing on problems, they focus on solutions.

The long-term impact of awareness is difficult to measure, but it is often one of the most important outcomes of volunteer-driven initiatives.
