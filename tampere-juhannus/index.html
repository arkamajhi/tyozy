A Small Event With A Bigger Purpose

On 19 June 2026, while many people across Finland were preparing for Juhannus celebrations, a small group of volunteers gathered in Tampere with a different objective.

Instead of focusing solely on the holiday itself, they chose to dedicate part of their day to improving public spaces and giving something back to the community.

The Tampere Juhannus Cleanup was built around a simple belief: cleaner cities are created by people who care enough to take action.

Although only four volunteers participated, the event was never about the size of the group.

It was about demonstrating that positive community action often begins with a handful of motivated individuals who are willing to contribute their time and energy toward a common goal.

Together, the volunteers collected approximately three kilograms of litter from public spaces in Tampere.

More importantly, they helped create awareness, encouraged conversations about environmental responsibility, and demonstrated that community engagement remains strong even during a national holiday.

The cleanup was supported by Tyozy, a cleaning and moving company that believes businesses can contribute positively to the communities they serve.

Supporting volunteer-led initiatives is one way of extending those values beyond commercial services.

While three kilograms may seem like a modest result, the true value of the event extends far beyond the amount of litter collected.

Every item removed from public spaces represents a small improvement to the environment and a reminder that shared spaces require shared responsibility.

Tavoitteena oli osoittaa, että yhteisöllinen vaikuttaminen voi alkaa pienestä ryhmästä motivoituneita ihmisiä.

Vapaaehtoiset keräsivät noin kuusi kiloa roskaa ja samalla lisäsivät tietoisuutta ympäristövastuusta sekä yhteisten alueiden merkityksestä.

Tapahtumaa tuki Tyozy, joka uskoo vahvasti yhteisöjen kehittämiseen ja ympäristövastuuseen.

Why We Organized The Tampere Juhannus Cleanup

Juhannus is one of Finland’s most important celebrations. Across the country, people gather with friends and family, spend time outdoors, travel to summer cottages, enjoy lakeside saunas, and celebrate the longest days of the year.

Public spaces play an important role in these celebrations. Parks, waterfronts, walking routes, beaches, and gathering places become locations where memories are created and communities come together.

Unfortunately, increased activity can sometimes result in increased litter.

The purpose of this cleanup was never to criticize celebrations or the people enjoying them.

Instead, the objective was to encourage a culture where everyone feels responsible for preserving the spaces they enjoy.

We wanted to create a positive event that focused on action rather than criticism.

Instead of asking why litter exists, we asked a different question:

“What can we do today to make our city a little better?”

The answer was simple.

Gather volunteers. Create awareness. Collect litter. Lead by example.

That philosophy became the foundation of the Tampere Juhannus Cleanup.

Halusimme keskittyä käytännön tekoihin.

Kysymys oli yksinkertainen: mitä voimme tehdä tänään tehdäksemme Tampereesta hieman paremman?

Vastaus oli tämä tapahtuma.

Why Community Cleanups Matter

Community cleanups are often viewed as simple volunteer activities, but their benefits extend far beyond waste collection.

At their core, community cleanups create visible examples of civic responsibility.

When residents actively care for shared spaces, they communicate a powerful message: this place matters.

People are more likely to respect clean environments, spend time outdoors, and feel connected to their communities.

Clean public spaces also contribute to physical and mental wellbeing.

Parks, trails, lakeshores, and gathering places become more attractive and enjoyable when they are free from litter.

Environmental benefits are equally important.

Items left in public areas can eventually enter waterways, break down into smaller particles, or negatively affect wildlife.

Community cleanups also strengthen social connections.

Volunteers who may never have met otherwise come together around a shared purpose.

The impact of a cleanup often continues long after the final bag of litter has been collected.

Miksi Yhteisölliset Siivoustapahtumat Ovat Tärkeitä

Yhteisölliset siivoustapahtumat eivät ole vain roskien keräämistä.

Ne lisäävät yhteisöllisyyttä, ympäristövastuuta ja ylpeyttä omasta kaupungista.

Siistit ympäristöt kannustavat ihmisiä käyttämään yhteisiä alueita ja pitämään niistä huolta myös tulevaisuudessa.

Why Tampere Is Worth Protecting

Tampere is one of Finland’s most vibrant and livable cities.

Located between Lake Näsijärvi and Lake Pyhäjärvi, the city combines natural beauty, industrial heritage, innovation, culture, and a strong sense of community.

Residents and visitors enjoy parks, waterfront routes, beaches, cycling paths, forests, and public gathering spaces throughout the year.

During the summer months these places become especially important.

Families spend afternoons in parks. Friends gather by the lakes. Students enjoy the long evenings. Visitors discover what makes Tampere unique.

The quality of these experiences depends heavily on the condition of public spaces.

A clean environment creates comfort, pride, and wellbeing.

Every piece of litter removed contributes to preserving the qualities that make Tampere special.

Community cleanups are therefore not only environmental projects. They are investments in the future of the city and the experience of everyone who uses its public spaces.

Miksi Tampereesta Kannattaa Pitää Huolta

Tampere tunnetaan järvistään, puistoistaan, kulttuuristaan ja vahvasta yhteisöllisyydestään.

Kesäisin kaupunkilaiset ja vierailijat käyttävät aktiivisesti puistoja, rantoja, kävelyreittejä ja muita yhteisiä alueita.

Näiden alueiden puhtaus vaikuttaa suoraan siihen, kuinka viihtyisäksi kaupunki koetaan.

Jokainen kerätty roska auttaa säilyttämään Tampereen vetovoiman ja viihtyisyyden.

Preparing For The Event

Successful community events rarely happen by accident.

Although the Tampere Juhannus Cleanup was intentionally simple and accessible, preparation still played an important role in ensuring the event ran smoothly.

Before volunteers arrived, event materials were prepared, banners were created, meeting locations were selected, and communication efforts were made to encourage participation.

One of the primary objectives during planning was to create a welcoming atmosphere.

Community initiatives are most successful when people feel comfortable participating regardless of their experience level, background, or age.

No special qualifications were required. No previous volunteer experience was needed. Participants only needed a willingness to contribute to a cleaner city.

This simplicity was intentional. Many people want to help their communities but assume they need extensive resources or expertise. Events like this demonstrate that meaningful contributions can be made through small, practical actions.

Environmental Impact Report

One of the easiest mistakes when evaluating a cleanup event is focusing exclusively on the amount of litter collected.

While approximately three kilograms of waste were removed during the Tampere Juhannus Cleanup, the true environmental impact extends far beyond a single number.

Every item removed from public spaces represents a potential environmental problem prevented.

Plastic waste left outdoors can break down into smaller particles and contribute to long-term pollution.

Food containers can attract wildlife and create sanitation issues.

Discarded waste can eventually enter drainage systems and waterways.

By removing litter before it remains in the environment for extended periods, volunteers help reduce these risks while improving the appearance and usability of shared public spaces.
